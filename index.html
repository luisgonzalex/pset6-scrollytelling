Interactive Migration Journeys

By: Tanner Bonner, Sol Rodriguez, Luis Gonzalez

Primary Question & Motivations



For our project, we are focusing telling the stories surrounding the financial burden faced by Northern Triangle migrants during and after they attempt to migrate.



Most migrations from the Northern Triangle are "irregular", and such a journey often poses more costs to a migrant than by regular means (which are not usually optional).



Additionally, contextual and personal factors (e.g. age, sex, location) and options available for their migration journey pose high risk of financial instability.



Irregular migration is costly for Northern Triangle migrants seeking financial security in the U.S.

We believe it is important that the stories of these migrants are empathetically shared to illustrate the financial barriers posed by the lack of a regular migration system between the U.S. and the Northern Triangle.

Wireframes

This is what we envision the landing page of our website to look like! We would like to map out the paths that migrants took to get to the US, and have checkpoints along the way that the user can interact with to learn about the journey. Some example checkpoints are motives for migration, navigating the southern Mexico border, crossing the US-Mexico border, and life in the US.

Mapbox Visualization



It is important for us that we put our audience in the shoes of the migrant stories we are following. One way that we propose to do that, is by having a mapbox with a journey outlined that traces over commons immigrant routes.



Like we mentioned in our wireframe, we'd like to include checkpoints that hightlight the major events during the journey. Part of what we envision for the mapbox, is that the audience is transported to these places and sees the long exodus that migrants must make in hope of a better future before reaching their destinations.



By outlining a specific path as a migrant moves from country to country and the checkpoints that show the costs associated with migration, we can tell their story more realistically and thus, effectively.
